PROBLEM STATEMENT: GENERATIVE AI RESEARCH PAPER STUDY AND IMPLEMENTATIONObjective:

The objective of this assignment is to bridge the gap between theoretical understanding and practical application of modern Generative AI techniques by critically analyzing a research paper and implementing its core ideas.Part 1: Research Paper Study and Presentation

Students are required to form groups of five and select a research paper relevant to Generative AI. The selected paper must go beyond standard textbook material and reflect current research trends in the field.Each group must:Clearly define the problem addressed in the paper.

Explain the motivation behind the work and limitations of existing approaches.

Describe the proposed methodology, including model architecture and key innovations.

Present the experimental setup, datasets used, and evaluation metrics.

Analyze the results and discuss their significance.

Identify limitations, assumptions, and potential areas for improvement.



The presentation should focus on building intuition and understanding rather than reproducing textbook definitions or generic explanations.Part 2: Implementation and Experimental Analysis

Based on the selected research paper, each group must implement the core methodology or a simplified version of it using appropriate tools and frameworks.The implementation phase must include:Reproducing key components of the model or system described in the paper.

Training and/or evaluating the model on a suitable dataset.

Comparing results with baseline models or expected outcomes.

Analyzing performance in terms of accuracy, efficiency, scalability, or other relevant metrics.

Identifying discrepancies between expected and observed results and providing possible explanations.



Students are encouraged to extend the original work by:Modifying the model architecture

Applying the method to a new dataset or domain

Improving performance or efficiency

Exploring novel use-cases



Deliverables:A structured presentation covering Part 1 (research understanding).

A live demonstration or recorded walkthrough of the implementation.

A summary of experimental findings, observations, and conclusions.



Evaluation Criteria:Depth of understanding of the research paper

Clarity and effectiveness of presentation

Correctness and completeness of implementation

Quality of experimental analysis and insights

Originality and effort in extending or improving the work



Constraints:Groups must consist of exactly five members.

Selected content must not duplicate textbook material.

Academic honesty and integrity must be strictly maintained.
